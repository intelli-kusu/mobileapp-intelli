About Intellicare Service and Eligibility

 

Patients can now get extended access to care at the comfort of their home, office, or wherever you are through the Intellicare app. Your medical group offers this service for its members' convenience at no additional cost to the patient. The fact that you have downloaded this app, you probably were directed to do so via text message or email, or by your primary doctor's office. If your medical group has not offered this service, check with the primary care physician or their office to sign up for the Intellicare service.  Alternatively, have an Intellicare staff check for your eligibility.  Click here to provide us the name of your group.

 

When would you use this virtual service?

If you think you're experiencing a medical emergency or a bodily injury, you should immediately seek emergency care by calling 911. Additionally, if you need to see your doctor for specific issues, you should contact your doctor's office

For most other non-critical illnesses (such as fever, cold, cough, rash, depression, and bodily aches), medication refill, therapeutic sessions, or medical questions, you can use this service. 

 

My doctor's offices asked me to use this service. How do I get started? 

If using the app for the first time, log in as directed. You must use your primary mobile number when logging in. This number is used for authentication and verified with a one-time password every time you log in. To ensure the provider has crucial information about the patient, you will need to enrol. Have your insurance card, a photo ID, and current medications. The app will walk you through a short but quick registration process. You can also reach out to our care coordinators to help you through this process. Once you have completed registration, the care coordinator will verify your insurance/medical group for eligibility.

 

What do I need to do to get care after I have registered?

Once the registration is complete, you can initiate a visit from the dashboard screen. If you are a repeat patient, you need to log in by using your name, DOB and registered phone number. After the One Time Password is verified, the app will take you right into the dashboard that allows you to initiate a visit with the medical provider.

 

What devices can I use to get my care?

Instead of a password, Intellicare validates a patient based sending a one-time password to their registered mobile phone. You can use your registered smart-phone (iPhone or Android phone) to get your virtual care. Alternatively, you may also download the Intellicare Health app on an iPad or tablet device and log in from that device but enter your registered mobile phone no to receive your one-time password.

 

Can my family use the Intellicare virtual care service?

If your group has signed on to Intellicare service, group members and dependents named in that insurance are eligible to receive virtual care. You will need to register them independently using the IntelliCare app because the provider will maintain a separate medical history for individual patients. If the dependent is an adult and has their mobile phone, they may use their mobile number when registering and continue to use that number for subsequent visits. 

 

Is this a 24x7 service?

Currently, providers will see you only from 6 AM to 10 PM all days of the week except Wednesdays. If you place a call outside these hours, your visit is in their queue, and the provider will call you when they are ready to see you. 

 

What is the wait time to see a provider?

Intellicare intends to patients with minimum wait time. When a patient initiates a visit, they are in the waiting room, that and an approximate wait time is displayed. When a provider is ready to see the patient, the provider sends a 3-minute notification to the app. The patient will have 3 minutes to initiate the call. Failure to start within the time allocated will result in the provider seeing the next patient. A cycle of notification will repeat. The provider might cancel the visit after repeated tries to engage with the patient. The patient also can withdraw the visit at any time when in the waiting room.

 

Do I have to turn on the video when talking to the provider?

Once connected to the provider, you will see the provider. The provider will hear you but not see you. You have the option of enabling the video, or the provider may request you to turn on the video for specific treatment purposes.
